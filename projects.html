My tattered clothes cling rather tightly to my frame, riddled with holes and smothered in filt. I take stock of my position. I lay on my back in a soup of foul smelling liquid that has all but seeped into my pores. I sit up and feel something in my pocket. My lighter. For once my chronic smoking habit comes in handy.
